William Jerome Scott

Cybersecurity Professional & Educator

About William

William Jerome Scott was born in Camden, New Jersey, in 1966 on a cold, snowy winter day. He began his education at Transfiguration Elementary School and later attended Camden Catholic High School in Cherry Hill.

In 1985, William enlisted in the United States Marine Corps, completing boot camp at Parris Island, South Carolina. He proudly served his country for 22 years, gaining extensive experience, leadership skills, and a deep sense of discipline and dedication.

Following his military service, William pursued higher education to complement his technical and leadership background. He earned an associate degree in Business and Computer Technology from Camden County College, studied Computer Science at Rowan University, completed his Bachelor of Arts at Thomas Edison University, and went on to receive a Master’s degree in Cybersecurity Technology from University of Maryland University College.

William’s professional career began as an Assistant Network Administrator at Merck in West Point, Pennsylvania. He then transitioned into education, teaching computer literacy as a substitute high school teacher. His commitment to public service led him to a role as the Federal Tuition Manager for the state of New Jersey, working on behalf of the federal government to support educational initiatives.

Currently, William serves as an Adjunct Computer Science Instructor at Gwinnett Technical College, where he shares his wealth of knowledge and experience with the next generation of IT professionals. His career reflects a lifelong dedication to service, education, and technological advancement.
